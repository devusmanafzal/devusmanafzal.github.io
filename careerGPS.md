# Career GPS Dashboard: Web App Brief

## Product Name

**Career GPS Dashboard**

## One-Line Positioning

**Know where you are today, so you can choose where to go next.**

## Purpose

Career GPS is a digital self-assessment tool that helps users understand their current personal and professional readiness across key growth areas. It should feel like a modern, guided dashboard rather than a traditional form or worksheet.

The tool helps users answer three questions:

1. Where am I today?
2. What is holding me back?
3. What should I focus on next?

The result should be a clean, visual, personalized dashboard that gives the user a practical starting point for their growth journey.

***

# Target Audience

Students, early professionals, career changers, and ambitious individuals who want more clarity, confidence, and direction in their personal and professional growth.

The tone should feel encouraging, calm, practical, and non-judgmental.

***

# Desired Look and Feel

Use a **minimal Nordic design style**.

Design direction:

* Clean white or very light gray background
* Soft muted colors: off-white, slate, cool gray, pale blue, muted teal
* Plenty of whitespace
* Rounded cards
* Simple icons
* Calm, premium, focused layout
* Modern typography, for example Inter, Segoe UI, or similar
* Avoid flashy gradients, heavy colors, or gamified visuals
* The experience should feel like a thoughtful personal dashboard, not a quiz app

Visual inspiration: calm productivity app, personal analytics dashboard, wellness assessment, modern education platform.

***

## 2. Assessment Flow

Create a multi-step assessment with progress indicator.

Suggested sections:

### Section 1: Direction

Measures clarity of goals and future path.

Questions:

* I have a clear idea of what I want to work toward in the next 12 months.
* I know which skills or capabilities I need to develop.
* I can describe what success looks like for me right now.
* I feel intentional about where I am heading.

### Section 2: Confidence

Measures self-belief and ability to act.

Questions:

* I feel confident talking about my strengths.
* I take action even when I do not have everything figured out.
* I can handle uncertainty without getting completely stuck.
* I believe I can create better opportunities for myself.

### Section 3: Personal Brand & Visibility

Measures how clearly the person can communicate value.

Questions:

* I can clearly explain what I am good at.
* My online or professional profile reflects my strengths.
* People around me understand the value I bring.
* I actively share my ideas, work, or progress in some way.

### Section 4: Professional Communication

Measures clarity and confidence in communication.

Questions:

* I can explain my ideas clearly.
* I can write professional messages with confidence.
* I can structure my thoughts when presenting or speaking.
* I adapt my communication depending on the audience.

### Section 5: AI Readiness

Measures confidence using AI practically.

Questions:

* I already use AI tools for learning, writing, planning, or work.
* I know how to write useful prompts.
* I know how to review and improve AI-generated outputs.
* I can see practical ways AI can help me grow.

### Section 6: Productivity & Consistency

Measures systems, habits, and execution.

Questions:

* I have a system for planning my work or studies.
* I follow through consistently on important tasks.
* I manage my time and energy intentionally.
* I use routines or workflows to reduce repeated effort.

Use a 1-5 rating scale:

1. No
2. Rarely
3. Sometimes
4. Often
5. Consistently

***

# Scoring Logic

Calculate a score for each dimension:

* Direction
* Visibility
* Capability
* Amplification

Each dimension is scored from 0 to 100.

Dimension score formula:

`(average question score / 5) * 100`

Overall Career GPS Score formula:

`(Direction + Visibility + Capability + Amplification) / 4`

Example:

* Direction = 80
* Visibility = 65
* Capability = 75
* Amplification = 60

Career GPS Score = 70

## 3. Strengths Card

Automatically identify the top two scoring categories.

Example:
**Your current strengths**

* Professional Communication
* Direction

## 4. Growth Focus Card

Automatically identify the lowest two scoring categories.

Example:
**Your recommended focus areas**

* Personal Brand & Visibility
* AI Readiness

## 5. Next Step Recommendation

Generate simple guidance based on the lowest category.

Examples:

If Visibility is lowest:  
**Focus on making your value more visible. Start by writing a one-sentence personal brand statement and updating your profile summary.**

If AI Readiness is lowest:  
**Start using AI for one practical task every week, such as planning, writing, summarizing, or learning.**

If Productivity is lowest:  
**Build a simple weekly planning routine and turn repeated tasks into reusable workflows.**
